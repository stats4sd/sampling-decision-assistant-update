To design a sampling plan, it is important to define the reporting requirements or goals. Depending on how you intend to report results, you may or may not have to consider your total sample size and method of selection differently.

The first question is to decide if you wish to divide your population to produce disaggregated estimates. If you need to give different figures for separate groups within your population, then you actually need an estimate for each one.

Here, think about the reporting requirements for your estimates or any stratification criteria that you need to include. Reporting requirements means that you will need to provide estimates for the indicator of interest for each subsection of the population, divided by criteria you specify.

Often strata are formed to define reporting requirements and in those cases this tool makes no difference between strata and reporting requirements. Sometimes, you may need to divide your population into strata to improve the precision of your estimates, but you have no requirement for estimates disaggregated by those strata. If you want this type of strata, make sure you remove the ‘reporting required’ tick from the table on the next screen where your reporting requirements and strata are summarised.

Provide a name or reference for the estimate. This should be the same as, or an abbreviation of your indicator
