Introduction

The purpose of the Sampling Decision Assistant is to provide a tool to support the design of sampling strategies among UNHCR staff when there is a need for primary data collection as mentioned in the UNHCR Needs Assessment Handbook. These operations often arise as part of needs assessment processes when not enough information is available from secondary sources. In those cases it is important to remember that the appropriateness and feasibility of different data collection methodologies will depend on:

  • The objectives of the primary data collection exercise
  • Constraints and limitations
  • The context
  • The expected benefits and costs

A careful choice of the data collection methods is important, and whatever the choice, each has advantages and disadvantages. If sampling is involved in the process, then you will need to decide whether is possible, this will produce a sample that is representative of the population. Alternatively, the information may come from a sample drawn using which will have limitations in so far as making inferences about the population. Either choice has consequences on how the resulting information can be used.
