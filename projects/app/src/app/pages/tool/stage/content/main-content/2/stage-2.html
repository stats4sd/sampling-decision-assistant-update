What indicators will guide your sampling decisions?

While most studies that use sampling intend to quantify multiple indicators , it is normal to base decisions about the study design on just one – a ‘key indicator’. In this step, you will be asked to choose the key indicator for your study and to specify some details about the estimate you are looking for, which will help you make sampling decisions later on.

If at all possible, try to select an indicator from the list of household indicators provided by the International Household Survey Network.

There are several considerations to make when selecting a key indicator. For guidance on this decision, please refer to the Additional Resources and the introduction video for this step.

The next question is about the estimate you are interested in. Many estimation processes are about estimating either of the following. Please select the estimate that best describes your interest.
